Bipolar disorder

  • This disorder affects your mood, energy, and activity levels. People with bipolar disorder can also be affected during their daily life. It sometimes makes daily task hard to complete. People with this brain disorders can be affected emotionally ranging from extremely high to extremely low.

  • Symptoms include:
    • Mood swings
    • Disorganized behavior
    • Lack of concentration
    • Manic episodes
    • Difficulty sleeping
    • Weight gain or loss

  • Treatments include:
    • Medication
        (ex: Olanzipine(Zyprexa), Ziprasidone(Geodon), Lurasidone(Latuda), etc


Schizophrenia

  • This disorder affects your ability to think clearly. Schizophrenia causes people to hallucinate and makes it hard to differentiate between reality and fantasy. People with schizophrenia often hear voices in there head. There is no set number to how many voices they hear. These voices can make it hard to make hard for them to make appropraite decisions.

  • Symptoms include:
    • Hallucinations
    • Delusions
    • Disorganized speech
    • Trouble concentrating

  • Treatments include:
    • Antidepressants
    • Anti-anxiety drugs
    • Antipsychotic drugs
    • A specialized treatment team


OCD

  • Obsessive-compulsive disorder is a mental illness that features unreasonable thoughts and fears that lead to repetitive behaviors. An example of OCD is a germaphobe who washes their hands until they are sore and chapped.

  • Symptoms include:
    • Fear of contamination
    • Needing things orderly and symmetrical
    • Aggressive or horrific thoughts about harming yourself or others
    • Intense stress when objects aren't orderly or facing a certain way

  • Treatments include:
    • Psychotherapy
      • Cognitive behavioral therapy
    • Medications
      • Clomipramine (Anafranil)
      • Fluoxetine (Prozac)
      • Fluvoxamine
      • Paroxetine (Paxil, Pexeva)
      • Setraline (Zoloft)


Eating Disorders

  • Eating disorders are serious conditions that are related to persistent eating behaviors that negatively impact lives. Most eating disorders come from focusing on weight, body shape, and food which lead to dangerous eating behaviors. Common eating disorders include anorexia, bulimia,and binge-eating.

  • Symptoms include:
    • Excessively eating or not eating

  • Treatments include:
    • Psychotherapy
      • Cognitive behavioral therapy
    • Teams
      • Primary care providers
      • Mental health professionals and dietians
